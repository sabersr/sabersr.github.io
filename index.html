Introduction

The Academic Award (Oscar) has always been criticized for its lack of diversity. The winners are usually from a homogeneous group that excludes minorities from winning the awards. On the other hand, in recent years, people claim that the idea of political correctness has influenced the choices of the winners. Previously underrepresented groups have been included in the Oscar community for various reasons.

As a result, we want to address the demographic stereotypes for Oscar winners and answer the question of whether race, religion or other demographic factors will influence the decision of the awards. Throughout history, it seems that the winners of Best Actor/Actress/Supporting Actor/Supporting Actress/ Director are predominantly white. Also, the winners for Best Director seem to be mostly males. By analyzing the winners throughout the years, we try to testify the veracity of this stereotype and to see if there is any improvement or degeneration of the inclusiveness of the award.

Technical Details

Installation

  • Make sure you have idyll installed (npm i -g idyll).

Developing a post locally

Run idyll.

Building a post for production

Run idyll build. The output will appear in the top-level build folder. To change the output location, change the output option in package.json.

Deploying

Make sure your post has been built, then deploy the docs folder via any static hosting service.
